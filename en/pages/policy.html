Privacy Policy.

January 1, 2024
1. Personal information we collect
We collect the following personal information for membership registration, consultations, and service requests.
Collection method: website (membership registration, bulletin boards, application forms).
1. Login ID
2. Password
3. Nickname
4. Email
5. Service usage records
6. Access logs
7. Cookies
8. IP address
9. Payment records
2. Purpose of collection and use
We use collected personal information for the following purposes.
1. Performance of contracts and fee settlement for service provision
- Providing content, shipping goods or sending invoices, identity verification, purchase and payment processing, debt collection
2. Member management
- Identity verification for member services, personal identification, prevention of improper or unauthorized use, confirmation of registration intent
3. Marketing and advertising
- Measuring access frequency or statistics on service use
3. Retention and use period
In principle, we destroy personal information without delay once the purpose of collection and use is achieved.
However, if retention is required by applicable laws, we keep member information for the period specified by those laws as follows.
1. Items retained: Login ID, payment records
2. Basis for retention: Act on Use and Protection of Credit Information
3. Retention period: 3 years
- Records on display/advertising: 6 months (Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc.)
- Records on contracts or withdrawal of subscription: 5 years (Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc.)
- Records on payment and supply of goods: 5 years (Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc.)
- Records on consumer complaints or dispute handling: 3 years (Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce, etc.)
- Records on collection/processing/use of credit information: 3 years (Act on Use and Protection of Credit Information)
4. Destruction procedure and method
We destroy personal information without delay once the purpose of collection and use is achieved. The procedure and method are as follows.
1. Procedure
- Information entered by members is moved to a separate database (or, for paper records, a separate file) after the purpose is achieved, and stored for a certain period in accordance with internal policies and related laws (see retention period), then destroyed. Personal information moved to a separate database is not used for any other purpose unless required by law.
2. Method
- Personal information stored in electronic files is deleted using technical methods that make the records unrecoverable.
5. Provision of personal information
We do not provide users' personal information to third parties without consent. Exceptions are as follows.
1. When the user has given prior consent
2. When required by laws or when investigative authorities request it in accordance with procedures and methods prescribed by law
6. Entrustment of processing
We do not outsource users' information to external companies without consent.
If such a need arises, we will notify users of the entrusted parties and tasks and obtain prior consent when necessary.
7. Rights of users and legal representatives, and how to exercise them
Users may view or update their personal information at any time and may request membership withdrawal.
To view or update personal information, use "Edit Profile" (or "Edit Member Info"). To withdraw consent or membership, click "Withdraw Membership" and complete identity verification to view, correct, or withdraw directly.
Alternatively, you may contact the privacy officer in writing or by email, and we will take action without delay.
If you request correction of errors, we will not use or provide the personal information until the correction is complete.
If incorrect personal information has already been provided to a third party, we will notify the third party of the correction without delay.
Personal information deleted at the user's request is processed according to the "Retention and use period" and will not be accessed or used for any other purpose.
8. Automatic collection devices (cookies) and how to refuse them
We use cookies that store and retrieve information from time to time.
Cookies are small text files sent to your browser by the server operating our website and stored on your computer's hard drive.
We use cookies for the following purposes.
1. Purpose of cookie use
- Analyze access frequency and visit time of members and non-members, identify user preferences and interests, track participation in events, and provide targeted marketing and personalized services
Users can choose whether to allow cookies. You can set your browser to allow all cookies, prompt each time a cookie is saved, or block all cookies.
2. How to refuse cookies
- You can choose browser options to allow all cookies, prompt each time a cookie is saved, or block all cookies.
Example (Internet Explorer): Tools > Internet Options > Privacy
If you refuse cookies, some services may not be available.
